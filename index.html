Denton A. Cooley, M.D. F.A.C.S.

"A successful surgeon should be a man who, when asked to name the three best surgeons in the world, would have difficulty deciding on the other two."

Here is a time line of Dr. Cooley's life:

  • 1920 - Born on August 26th, in Houston, to Ralph Clarkson Cooley and Mary Fraley Cooley. Denton was born to a line of successful men; his father was a dentist and his grandfather was a major developer and founder of the Houston Heights neighborhood.
  • 1941 - Graduates from University of Texas with a major in zoology.
  • 1944 - Graduates from Johns Hopkins School of Medicine with a medical degree. Dr. Cooley assists Dr. Alfred Blalock in the first blue-baby operation.
  • 1948 - Deneton joins and serves the Army Medical Corp as captain and chief of surgery for two years.
  • 1949 - Marries the lovely Louise Goldborough Thomas who also serves in the medical field as a registered nurse. Together they build a small family by adding five daughters.
  • 1951 - Serves 18 years as a professor of surgery at Baylor College of Medicine under Dr. Michael DeBakey.
  • 1962 - Founds his own private surgical practice, Texas Heart Institute at St. Luke’s Episcopal Hospital, which eventually becomes one of the top academic heart centers in the United States.
  • 1968 - Performs world's first human heart transplant where the patient lasted several weeks. Christiaan Barnard performed the first transplant in South Africa months earlier, however, the patient survived less than three weeks. Cooley’s survived 204 days.
  • 1969 - Implants first patient received an artificial heart, creating a masssive feud with fellow surgeon DeBakey. The device, similar (if not identical) to one in DeBakey’s laboratory, was a temporary fix until a donor heart was found three days later; the patient died less than two days after receiving the heart.
  • 1984 - Former President Ronald Reagan presented Cooley with the National Medal of Freedom for his great contributions to the medical field.
  • 1998 - Former President Bill Clinton presented Dr. Cooley with the National Medal of Technology.
  • 2007 - Makes amends with former fellow surgeon DeBakey.
  • 2016 - Dr. Denton A. Cooley passes away on November 18th at the age of 96.
